# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Project Managers tracking Estimate at Completion (EAC) across construction and infrastructure projects. Desk-based, daylight hours, concentrated work. Period-end reviews, variance acknowledgment, cost forecasting, resource management. Secondary users: Finance team running revenue recognition and PD approval workflows.

Primary job: understand financial status quickly, act on exceptions, close periods with confidence.

## Product Purpose

PFMS (Project Financial Management System) is an enterprise tool for managing project financials end-to-end: EAC editing, portfolio health, revenue recognition, purchase director approvals, resource planning, SAP integration, and period locking. It replaces spreadsheet-and-email workflows with structured, role-aware screens.

Success: a PM can open the app, see what needs attention today, act on it, and close the period without leaving to email, SAP, or a spreadsheet.

## Brand Personality

Warm precision. Quietly authoritative. The kind of tool a competent professional trusts without thinking about it.

Three words: precise, warm, focused.

Emotional goal: confidence. The PM should feel in control of their numbers, not fighting the interface to extract them.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS / Salesforce: blue-heavy, corporate overload, sidebar navigation that lists 40 items.
- Excel / SAP: dense grid-everything, no visual hierarchy, every cell the same weight as every other.
- Startup dashboard templates: gradient metric cards, hero numbers in 72px type, dark mode with neon accents.

## Design Principles

1. **Status at a glance.** Every screen must communicate what needs attention within 2 seconds. Exception = red. On track = quiet. Critical path items surface themselves.

2. **Dense but structured.** Financial management is inherently data-dense. Embrace it through typographic hierarchy and spatial rhythm, not by hiding data behind tabs and drawers.

3. **Warmth earns trust.** The warm neutral palette is not decoration. It signals that a human designed this for humans, not that a vendor shipped another enterprise product. Protect the warmth; it's the differentiator.

4. **Workflow-native defaults.** UI patterns follow the PM's mental model: period → projects → WBS → cost items. Don't impose a generic app shell on a domain-specific workflow.

5. **Precision in copy and numbers.** Every label, status, and number is exact. No approximate language, no filler microcopy. The numbers are the product.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Minimum 4.5:1 contrast for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components. Full keyboard navigation for all workflows. Status not conveyed by color alone (dot + label for all status indicators).
